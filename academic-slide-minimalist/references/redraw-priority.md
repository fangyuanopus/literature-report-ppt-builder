# Redraw Priority

Use this reference after deck diagnosis to avoid unnecessary full-deck regeneration.

## Priority Classes

### A: Must Redraw

Redraw the image2 page or rebuild it from source materials when:

- the scientific figure is fake, AI-generated, altered, or not traceable;
- the page lacks required navigation or page number;
- the title and figure do not support each other;
- the main figure is unreadable in presentation mode;
- page style clearly differs from the approved deck style;
- old draft pages are mixed into the final deck;
- the page belongs in a different section and cannot be locally fixed;
- the page cannot support a clear 30-60 second speaker note.

### B: Local Repair

Repair without full redraw when:

- the title is vague but the figure and layout are usable;
- key trends or numbers need red boxes, labels, or short callouts;
- text is too long but can be shortened;
- figure crop can be adjusted without changing the layout;
- navigation highlight or page number needs correction;
- the page should be moved but its visual design is otherwise acceptable.

### C: Keep

Keep the page when:

- the figure is real and traceable;
- navigation, page number, and section are correct;
- the conclusion title matches the evidence;
- the key trend is visible;
- style is consistent with the rest of the deck;
- the slide has a clear speaker-note path.

## Redraw Plan Template

```text
slide | priority A/B/C | reason | action | source figure(s) | target output filename
```

Start with A pages. Do not spend time redrawing C pages unless the user explicitly asks for a complete visual refresh.
