# Question Preparation

Use this reference to prepare for real literature-report discussion and teacher/advisor questions.

## Purpose

A good literature-report PPT should be speakable and defensible. For important slides, prepare likely questions and safe answers grounded in the paper and SI.

## Required Question Types

For maximum-quality runs, prepare questions covering:

1. research motivation: why this problem matters;
2. novelty: what is actually new compared with prior work;
3. method validity: why the method proves the claim;
4. figure interpretation: what the key trend or panel shows;
5. controls: whether SI includes control experiments;
6. mechanism: whether the mechanism is proven or proposed;
7. performance comparison: whether conditions are fair;
8. stability/reproducibility: whether long-term or repeated tests exist;
9. limitations: what the paper has not solved;
10. transferable insight: what can be learned for future work.

## Question-Answer Template

```text
slide:
likely question:
safe answer:
source support:
caution:
```

Example:

```text
slide: 14
likely question: 这个孔径分布能否证明孔道来自晶体骨架本身？
safe answer: 该图支持本征介孔判断，但需要与结构模型和显微观察共同理解，不能单独依靠吸附结果证明完整晶体结构。
source support: Fig. 4A-B plus structural figures
caution: avoid saying adsorption alone fully proves the framework.
```

## Answer Style

- Answer in cautious academic Chinese.
- Distinguish what the paper proves from what it suggests.
- Mention SI support when available.
- Acknowledge limitations instead of over-defending weak claims.
- Do not invent experiments that the paper did not perform.

## Deliverable

When feasible, create `possible_questions.md` or include a question section in `speaker_notes.md`.

## Risk Level Link

Use `question-risk-level.md` to classify key slide claims as low, medium, or high risk. High-risk claims need backup support, cautious wording, and a safe answer. Do not defend high-risk claims with invented experiments.
