# Terminology Control

Use this reference to keep Chinese literature-report slides consistent, academically natural, and faithful to the paper.

## When to Use

Use for every full paper-to-PPT task, especially when the paper contains many technical terms, sample names, abbreviations, material names, reaction names, or measurement methods.

## Required Terminology Table

Before writing slide copy, create or internally maintain a terminology table:

```text
English term | Chinese term | use rule | avoid / caution
```

Examples:

```text
framework | 骨架 | use for zeolite/material framework | do not alternate randomly with “框架” unless context needs it
mesopore | 介孔 | 2-50 nm pore range | do not confuse with macropore
adsorption | 吸附 | surface/pore uptake | do not translate as absorption
oxidation | 氧化 | general oxidation reaction | distinguish from epoxidation
suggests | 表明/提示 | use cautious wording | do not translate as “证明”
```

## Naming Rules

- Preserve material/sample names exactly unless the paper provides an accepted Chinese form.
- Preserve abbreviations such as PXRD, XRD, SEM, TEM, STEM, ADF-STEM, NMR, XPS, BET, DFT, TOF, TON when they are standard.
- Preserve units exactly and do not change unit systems unless explicitly converting with explanation.
- Keep Greek letters, chemical formulas, oxidation states, and charge labels accurate.
- Do not simplify sample names in a way that makes comparison unclear.

## Translation Tone

Use clear Mainland Chinese academic presentation language. Avoid machine-translation tone and avoid overly literary language.

Prefer:

- 本文报道/构建/验证……
- 该结果表明……
- 与对照样品相比……
- 这一差异可能源于……
- 需要注意的是……

Avoid:

- 赋能、平台化、范式革新, unless the paper truly uses and supports such language;
- broad slogans such as “应用前景广阔” without evidence;
- mixing multiple translations for the same technical term across slides.

## Claim Verb Calibration

Map English verbs carefully:

```text
demonstrates -> 证明/表明, depending on evidence strength
shows -> 显示/表明
indicates -> 表明/说明
suggests -> 提示/支持/可能表明
may/could -> 可能
is consistent with -> 与……一致
is attributed to -> 作者将其归因于……
```

When uncertain, choose a weaker Chinese verb.

## Final Check

Before final delivery, check:

- key terms are translated consistently;
- sample names are unchanged;
- figure labels in the slide match the source figure;
- conclusion titles do not use stronger wording than the paper;
- speaker notes use the same terminology as slides.
