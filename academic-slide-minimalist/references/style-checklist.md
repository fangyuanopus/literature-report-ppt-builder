# Academic Slide Minimalist Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing a page or full deck.

## Full Deck

- Does the deck follow the user's sample PPT structure instead of a newly invented structure?
- Is the full literature-report deck at least 20 pages unless the user requested fewer?
- Does the deck explain the paper logic clearly rather than compressing too much content?
- Were both the main paper and supplementary information read when both were supplied?
- Was rigorous close reading used to avoid mistranslation, missing experimental details, and unsupported claims?
- Are the main sections complete: 基本信息, 研究背景, 研究思路, 研究结果, 总结启发?
- Are navigation tabs, section labels, page numbers, margins, and red-black-gray academic style consistent?

## Paper Logic

- Has the paper logic been parsed before slide design?
- Is the logic tree grounded in the original paragraphs, figures, and SI support rather than vague summary?
- Can the story be summarized as background -> problem -> strategy -> evidence -> performance -> conclusion?
- Does each result slide answer one clear question?
- Are limitations and preliminary evidence described cautiously?

## Figure Source Integrity

- Is every scientific visual from the paper, supplementary information, user-provided PPT, or user-provided screenshot?
- Does the figure manifest identify main-paper versus SI sources?
- Are there no invented structures, spectra, microscopy images, curves, tables, mechanisms, or data charts?
- Has each used figure been mapped to the slide claim?
- Are cropped figures still scientifically faithful and not misleading?
- Are figure annotations simple and interpretive rather than decorative?

## Message

- Can the slide's conclusion be understood within 3 seconds?
- Does the title state a result, contradiction, or judgment?
- Is there only one main message?
- Does the slide avoid repeating the same conclusion in multiple boxes?

## Figure Layout

- Is there one dominant figure or one clearly dominant evidence block?
- Is the key region/sample/trend directly annotated?
- Are unnecessary panels removed, cropped, moved to backup, or visually weakened?
- Does the figure occupy enough space to be readable in presentation mode?

## Text

- Is the text reduced to one conclusion plus at most 2-3 short points?
- Are long paragraphs removed?
- Are keywords large and easy to scan?
- Are captions interpretive rather than purely descriptive?

## Design

- Is the background plain white or very light gray?
- Are red, black, and gray used consistently and sparingly?
- Are decorative icons, heavy shadows, busy gradients, glows, 3D effects, and commercial template elements avoided?
- Is there enough whitespace?

## Final PPT Assembly

- Were 16:9 image2 pages generated first?
- Were image2 pages inserted full-slide into the PPT?
- Does the final PPT preserve layout stability with no shifted elements?


## Sample Deck Consistency

- Does the deck follow the bundled/user sample's section rhythm: 封面, 基本信息, 研究背景, 研究思路, 研究结果, 总结启发, 汇报完毕?
- Is the top navigation consistent with the sample deck's tab language?
- Are figure captions and page numbers visually consistent across pages?
- Is the sample deck used only as style/structure reference, not as scientific source?

## Quality Gates

Before final delivery, apply `references/quality-gates.md`.

## Terminology and Presentation Readiness

- Are key terms translated consistently across slides and notes?
- Are sample names, units, chemical formulas, and method abbreviations preserved?
- Are likely questions prepared for major claims and weak evidence?
- Are backup slides or backup plans available for dense SI evidence?
- Does the quality report include unresolved limitations and a final score when maximum-quality mode is used?


## Adaptive Navigation and Order

- Were navigation labels derived from the current paper instead of blindly copying a fixed label set?
- Are labels short, consistent, and useful for the audience?
- Does every content slide have the same navigation labels and the correct active highlight?
- Does `deck_order_map.md` define final page order before PPT assembly?
- Are mainline and backup pages clearly separated?
- Were all final slides copied into one approved final image2 folder before assembly?
- Was the assembled PPT audited as a whole with a montage or equivalent page review?
