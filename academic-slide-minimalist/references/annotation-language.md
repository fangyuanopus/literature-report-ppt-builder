# Annotation Language

Use this reference when writing labels, callouts, and emphasis text on scientific figures.

## Core Rule

Annotations must point to concrete evidence, not praise the result. Keep them short, precise, and useful for oral explanation.

## Good Annotation Types

Use annotations that name:

- the key sample;
- the key condition;
- the key number;
- the key peak or band;
- the key time region;
- the key before/after contrast;
- the key trend or disappearance/emergence.

Examples:

```text
5500 h stable
99% selectivity
near-equilibrium conversion
bridge CO bands disappear
Rh-In coordination
In/Rh >= 4
induction period
aggregation suppressed
```

Chinese examples:

```text
诱导期缩短
桥式 CO 峰消失
Rh-In 配位出现
转化率接近平衡值
长周期无明显失活
```

## Avoid Vague Labels

Avoid:

```text
excellent performance
important result
significant improvement
novel mechanism
very stable
best catalyst
效果很好
显著提升
核心发现
```

Unless the annotation also says exactly what improved and by how much.

## Placement Rules

- Place labels near the relevant region, not in a decorative corner.
- Do not cover axes, legends, peaks, scale bars, or data points.
- Use a limited number of annotations per slide, usually 1-3.
- Keep labels consistent across pages in wording and color.

## Speaker Link

Each annotation should help the presenter say one clear sentence:

```text
这里重点看...，它说明...
```
