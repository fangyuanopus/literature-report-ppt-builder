# Speaker Notes to Slide Check

Use this reference to test whether a slide is actually presentable.

## Core Rule

If a slide cannot support a clear 30-60 second spoken explanation, the slide is not ready even if it looks visually polished.

## Reverse Check Template

For each important slide, write or mentally verify:

```text
slide:
spoken purpose:
what the figure shows:
why it matters:
transition from previous slide:
transition to next slide:
risk or cautious wording:
backup support:
```

## Failure Signals

A slide needs repair when:

- the speaker note only repeats the title;
- the presenter cannot explain why the figure is on the slide;
- the figure and title do not support each other;
- the slide has too many claims for one minute;
- the transition to the next page is unclear;
- the claim requires a backup slide but none exists;
- the slide uses strong wording but the note must walk it back.

## Repair Actions

Use one of:

```text
rewrite title | reduce text | enlarge figure | annotate key trend | split page | move to backup | weaken claim | add backup support
```

## Minimum Speaker Note

At least key slides should have:

```text
30-60 second script:
transition sentence:
likely question:
safe answer:
```
