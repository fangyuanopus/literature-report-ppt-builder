# Figure Readability Standard

Use this reference before accepting any image2 page with scientific figures.

## Core Rule

A real figure is not useful if it is too small to read during presentation. Preserve scientific authenticity and presentation readability at the same time.

## Minimum Standards

### Charts and Spectra

- Axes, units, legends, sample names, and key labels must remain legible.
- Do not shrink a chart below the point where curves, peaks, or bars cannot be distinguished.
- Key peak positions or diagnostic regions may be marked, but annotations must not cover the data.
- If the whole figure is too dense, crop the relevant panel or split it across slides.

### Microscopy and Imaging

- Preserve scale bars whenever possible.
- Do not crop out labels needed to know the sample or condition.
- Mark only the region needed for the slide claim.
- If multiple images show the same trend, use the clearest one in mainline and move extras to backup.

### Tables

- Mainline tables should usually show only key rows and key columns.
- If a table has more than about 8 readable rows or too many columns, put the full table in backup.
- Highlight the row for this work and 2-4 representative comparison rows.
- Do not use a full dense table as a mainline slide unless the table itself is the main evidence.

### Multi-Panel Figures

- If more than 4 panels are equally important, split the figure across slides.
- If only one or two panels support the claim, crop those panels and cite the full figure ID.
- Preserve panel letters when they are referenced in text or notes.

### DFT / Mechanism / Energy Figures

- Preserve energy values, axes, reaction labels, and legends.
- Mark only the decisive barrier, trend, or comparison.
- Do not redraw energy profiles or molecular structures unless they are real source figures.

## Fail Actions

If readability fails:

```text
crop | enlarge | split across slides | move full figure to backup | replace with a clearer real panel | use text explanation with source noted
```
